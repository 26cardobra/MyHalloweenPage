Here are some Halloween Fact:

  • The fear of Halloween is called Samhainophobia, while the fear of candy is called Nonsense.

  • John Carpenter’s 1978 horror film, “Halloween,” was filmed in 21 days.
  • Many stores ban masks on Halloween to prevent robberies but require them due to COVID-19. Choose your mask wisely, I suppose.
  • About ¼ of annual candy sales in the US are for Halloween.
  • 20% of millennials bought costumes for their pets in 2018. Why? Because it’s adorable.
  • Fears of Halloween candy being poisoned are unfounded, as the few incidents over the years were found to be family-related crimes.
  • Candy corn was originally called “chicken feed.” Now it is just called gross.
  • Finding a spider on Halloween night is considered good luck, unless it’s in your shoe. That’s just scary.
  • The first Jack-O’-Lanterns were carved from turnips because no one wants to eat turnips.
